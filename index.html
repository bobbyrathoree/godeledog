FIRST, MOVE THE DISTURBANCE

“Unprovable” is not absolute. It means: unreachable from these axioms, by these rules.

Gödel did not fail to prove his theorem. He proved—using reasoning outside a chosen formal system—that the system has an inside edge.

The experience below lets you build that edge, turn arithmetic into a mirror, and watch every attempted escape redraw the horizon.

Give arithmetic a border.

Call the system T. Its axioms can be mechanically listed, its rules are exact, and it contains enough arithmetic to represent computations about encoded formulas and proofs. Assume it is consistent.

WHAT THE VISUAL COMPRESSES

The machinery beneath the mirror.

  1. 01
    EFFECTIVE AXIOMS

    T’s axioms are mechanically enumerable and its proofs mechanically checkable.

  2. 02
    ARITHMETIZATION

    Finite symbols, formulas, and proofs receive Gödel numbers.

  3. 03
    DIAGONALIZATION

    A fixed-point sentence G says that no number codes a T-proof of G.

  4. 04
    THE COLLISION

    If consistent T proved G, it would certify the very proof G denies.

  5. 05
    THE LIMIT

    Rosser’s refinement constructs a sentence R for which consistency alone rules out both R and ¬R.

  6. 06
    CONSISTENCY

    For a suitable consistent T and its standard provability predicate, T cannot prove its own formal consistency statement Con(T).

THE HYPOTHESES MATTER

Consistency, effective axiomatization, and enough elementary arithmetic are essential.

“TRUE” NEEDS A VIEWPOINT

Truth here refers to the standard natural numbers. Provability is always relative to a named system.

NOT THE LIAR PARADOX

G says it has no proof in T; it does not simply say “I am false.”

NO VIEW FROM NOWHERE

A formalized metatheory strong enough for arithmetic inherits a boundary of its own.

WHICH COMPLETENESS FAILS?

Deductive completeness would make T prove φ or ¬φ for every sentence φ. That is different from Gödel’s semantic completeness theorem for first-order logic.

PROVABILITY IS NOT TRUTH

Provability is arithmetically definable. No single arithmetical formula correctly defines truth for every arithmetical sentence; that is why the liar cannot be run through T.
